starburst-AGN



This project is led by Dr. Damian Mast from the Observatory of Córdoba. I'm a co-leader from the "Responsible Group". In this project, we study the spectroscopic and photometric properties of a sample of active and inactive galaxies, of all types, in the optical and in the infrared, from spatial scales of tens of parsecs to the largest interactions. The goal is to explore the connection between star formation and nuclear activity, a problem historically known as the starburst-AGN connection. Several contribution are produced in this group, including:

- NGC 2992: NIR variability in NGC 2992: Project leader: Lic. Selene Levis (IATE, UNC). I supported data reduction of images in J, H, and Ks, and LS spectroscopy in K-band, obtained between 2021 and 2022 with Flamingos-2 at Gemini South. This work is to be submitted for publication. Partial results are reported in Levis et al. 2023 https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2023BAAA...64..247L/abstract.

- NGC 253: Nuclear Stellar Clusters in NGC 253: Project leader: Dr. Javier Camperi (OAC, UNC). I contributed with the detection of sources with Source-Extractor, on J, H and Ks images from Flamingos-2. This work is under review for publication

- NGC 5128: GNIRS IFS of NGC 5128: Project leader, Dr. Ruben Díaz (Gemini Observatory). My contributions include: archive image compilation, WCS control, image alignment with IFU and LS fields of view, and PSF matching. This work is to be submitted for publication. Partial results were reported in Diaz, R.J., et al. (2021) https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2021BAAA...62..219D/abstract
