1- Evidence of the escape of ionizing photons from young compact HII regions [Interstellar Medium - Star-forming regions] (Collaboration with ASTRO-3D) Abstract: The ionizing photons of the stars that populated the first galaxies contributed to the progressive reionization of hydrogen from the early Universe. This cosmic process took place during the period known as Epoch of Reionization (EoR) of hydrogen. At the end of the EoR, all the hydrogen in the intergalactic medium (IGM) was ionized and has remained in this atomic state for the last ~13 billion years.The EoR modified the physical state of the IGM. As a result, during that time galaxies grew and formed under conditions different from the current ones. For this reason and for the cosmological importance of the EoR, understanding the cosmic process of reionization is one of the main objectives of modern astrophysics. One of the main unknowns is the number of ionizing photons that manage to escape from the galaxies during the EoR, which is usually expressed in terms of "escape fraction" of photons from the Lyman series continuum of hydrogen (LyC). In this project, we study three compact HII regions of the Small Milky Cloud (SMC) with integrated field spectroscopy (IFS) obtained with GMOS-S at Gemini South. The goals are: to obtain 3D maps and emission line ratios, to evaluate the effect of the geometry and the structure of each HII region in the escape of ionizing photons, and to infer properties such as metallicity and ionization state with the spatial resolution provided by the IFS technique. Description: Publications: Díaz, C.G., et al. (2023, BAAA, 64, 127) https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2023BAAA...64..127D/abstract
